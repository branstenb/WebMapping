Islands of the Maldives

Map 1: Malé, Maldives

Malé is the capital city of the Maldives, an archipelagic country southwest of India and Sri Lanka in the Indian Ocean. Malé is the 23rd most densely populated city in the world; its population of 211,908 (2022) resides mainly on 0.75 square miles of the urban main island. The main island is connected to the island of Hulhulé by the Sinamalé bridge, completed in 2018. Hulhulé contains Velana International Airport but has no permanent population.

Map 2: Maafushi, Maldives

Maafushi is a small island of about 2,500 people in the Maldives, mainly sustained by tourism. Just 16 miles away from Malé, it contains Maafushi prison, the largest prison in the Maldives, which has previously held multiple former presidents.

Map 3: Thoddoo, Maldives

Thoddoo is another small island of the Maldives, home to about 1,500 people and located 42 miles from Malé. Unlike many of the other islands, agriculture has a large presence on Thoddoo. Farmers grow various tropical fruits, including papaya, honey melon, passion fruit, banana, mango, and watermelon.
